Crop Price Variation

Wheat prices can vary for a variety of reasons. Some factors that can affect wheat prices include supply and demand, weather conditions, and government policies. For example, if there is a drought that reduces the wheat crop, the price of wheat may increase due to the decreased supply. On the other hand, if there is a surplus of wheat, the price may decrease. Additionally, government policies and trade agreements can also impact wheat prices. Overall, the price of wheat is determined by a combination of factors that can change over time.
